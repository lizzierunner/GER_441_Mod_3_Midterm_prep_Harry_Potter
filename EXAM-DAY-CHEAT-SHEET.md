## üî• MUST-KNOW Concepts

### Lin's "Princess as Reward"
**Definition:** Marriage to the prince is the ultimate reward for the heroine's suffering and virtue.

**Apply to Cinderella:**
- Grimm (1812): Suffering ‚Üí Marriage ‚Üí Happily Ever After
- Disney 1950: Beauty + Kindness ‚Üí Recognition ‚Üí Prince
- 2015 Live Action: More depth, but still ends with marriage

**Apply to Harry Potter:**
- REWARD SHIFTS: Romance ‚Üí Belonging & Identity
- Harry wants family, not princess
- Hogwarts = home, friends = reward

## üìñ Key Definitions

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Archetype** | Universal pattern, narrative-driven | Hero's journey, wise mentor |
| **Stereotype** | Rigid cultural expectation | "Princesses must be beautiful" |
| **Agency** | Character's choices affect outcome | Hermione solving puzzles |
| **Passive Rescue** | Being saved without action | Cinderella waiting for prince |
| **Active Rescue** | Taking action to save self/others | HP trio fighting Voldemort |

## üìù Essay Formula (Use This!)

### Structure:
1. **Intro** (3 sentences)
   - Restate question
   - Name all films
   - Preview argument

2. **Body** (3 paragraphs)
   - Para 1: Grimm/1950 evidence
   - Para 2: 2015 evidence + limitations
   - Para 3: HP evidence + comparison

3. **Conclusion** (2-3 sentences)
   - Summarize comparison
   - Final insight

### Magic Phrases:
- "According to Lin..."
- "This demonstrates..."
- "In contrast to..."
- "For example, when Hermione..."
- "This shift from X to Y shows..."
